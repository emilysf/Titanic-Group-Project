History

The idea of Titanic was first thought up in 1907 by J. Bruce Ismay and Lord Pirrie of the White Star Line. The Titanic was one of three ships planned: The Olympic, Titanic and Gigantic, which was later named Britannic. The three ships had almost identical builds. The Olympic had the longest life of the three ships, having its maiden voyage on June 14, 1911.

During World War I the Olympic surved as a troop transport. It was attacked 3 times by German U-Boats and survived each time. In May 1934, the Olympic accidentally hit and sunk the Nantucket Lightship, which killed 7 people. October 11, 1935 was the last time the Olympic sailed the ocean, having sailed for 24 years.

The Britannic's maiden voyage was originally planned for November of 1914 but was pushed back to the following spring due to the Titanic disaster. The Britannic was converted into a hospital that carried nearly 15,000 wounded soldiers from the war. On it's sixth trip the ship hit a mine, near the Greek island Kea, that caused the boat to sink in 55 minutes. Out of 1100 people on board only 30 died because their lifeboat was dragged into the spinning of the sinking Britannic. To this day the Britannic is the largest ship to lie on the ocean bottom; it lies in one piece about 400 feet below surface level. There is a plan in the future to turn the ship into an underwater museum. ​

The Titanic













On July 30, 1908, authorization was given to build the Titanic, and on March 31, 1909 construction started. April 2, 1912 Titanic was launched for sea trials, which includes tests of speed, turns, and an emergency stop. After, the Titanic headed to Southampton, England arriving on April 3rd.

Between April 3-10 supplies were loaded and crew members were hired. On April 10, 1912 from 9:30 am to 11:30 am, passengers boarded the ship. At noon the ship left the dock on its maiden voyage. The first stop was in Cherbourg, France, where the Titanic arrived at 6:30 p.m.and left at 8:10 p.m, heading towards Queenstown, Ireland. The ship arrived on April 11 and at 1:30 pm, the ship left for New York. On April 14, 1912 at approximately 11:40 pm, the ship strikes the iceberg that ultimately sunk the ship on April 15, 1912 at 2:20 am.​
